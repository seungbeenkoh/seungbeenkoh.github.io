About

I am an undergraduate student in Biochemistry at Yonsei University with hands-on research experience in protein structural biochemistry, particularly in GPCR and receptor-ligand systems. My work has centered on membrane protein expression and purification, peptide structural analysis by NMR, and preliminary cryo-EM-related workflows, with the broader goal of connecting molecular recognition to therapeutic discovery. Through additional experience in AI-assisted drug discovery and chromatin biology, I have developed a strong experimental foundation and a clear motivation to pursue Ph.D. training in structural and mechanistic biochemistry.

Selected Projects

Research

My research experience is centered on biochemical and structural approaches to molecular recognition, especially in GPCR systems, and is complemented by exposure to translational drug discovery and chromatin biology. The projects below represent the core experiences that define my current research direction and technical foundation.

GPCR structural biochemistry

Human UR2R/hU2 complex expression, purification, and preliminary cryo-EM characterization

Led the core experimental workflow for expression, purification, and complex preparation of ligand-bound human urotensin 2 receptor. Built the UR2R(11-336)-hU2-mGqi-scFv16 signaling complex for preliminary cryo-EM characterization, which resulted in a first-author publication in Protein Expression and Purification.

NMR-based peptide and receptor recognition analysis

Somatostatin analog structural analysis and receptor selectivity

Led a first-author poster project investigating the structural basis of somatostatin receptor selectivity by integrating NMR spectral analysis, peptide structure calculation, receptor-ligand interaction analysis, sequence alignment, structural visualization, and docking-based interpretation.
